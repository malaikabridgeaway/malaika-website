About Us

Who We Are

A community-driven non-governmental organization dedicated to improving the lives of vulnerable and underserved communities.

Malaika Bridge Away Foundation works through education support, access to clean water, digital learning opportunities and youth talent development. We believe every child and young person deserves:

  • Access to quality education
  • Safe and clean water
  • Opportunities to discover their talents
  • A supportive environment to grow and succeed

Our work focuses on empowering communities with practical solutions that create long-term impact — founded on the belief that the place where one is born should never determine destiny, turning the simple act of showing up for a community into lasting, generational change.

Malaika does not work around communities; it works with them, turning local knowledge into lasting solutions.

📍 Where we are: based in Sumve, Kwimba District, Mwanza Region — on the shores of Lake Victoria — with impact extending to communities in need across Tanzania.
Vision

What must be

Empowered and resilient communities where every learner has equal access to quality education, opportunity, and hope.

Mission

How we get there

To mobilize resources, partnerships, and community participation to improve learning conditions and community wellbeing through clean water projects, digital access, educational support, and talent development initiatives.

Objectives

Six connected goals

Support schools lacking essential infrastructure · improve access to clean water and sanitation · expand digital learning · strengthen youth empowerment and creativity · mobilize sustainable partnerships · promote education and community development.

What We Do

Our Programs

Infrastructure and human potential together — lasting impact is only possible when both are met.

💧

Clean Water Projects

We support the construction of wells and improved access to safe water. Clean water is the silent multiplier of every development goal: when a child no longer spends hours collecting water, those hours become classroom hours — and classroom hours become future.

💻

Digital Learning Support

Computers, digital devices, learning materials and access support for schools and students. In a world where digital fluency is as essential as reading and writing, access to technology is not a luxury — it is an act of equity.

🎭

Malaika Talent Development

Through our Talent Club we nurture singing, dancing, acting, comedy, fashion and creative arts — safe spaces where young people build confidence and skills. The Talent Club is where dreams first learn to speak out loud.

Priority Project · Bumyengeja, Sumve Ward

Clean Water for Two Schools and a Community

When you are thirsty, but clean water is far away

Today, students and families secure their daily water from shallow, open ponds that are highly vulnerable to contamination — or walk to sources far from home:

  • The nearest water sources are 1.5–3 km away — 45 minutes to 2 hours per trip
  • Households make 2–4 trips every day
  • Teachers report direct impact on absenteeism, performance and school health

Our mandate is clear: replace these dangerous, provisional sources with a protected community borehole with manual or solar pumping and community distribution points, inside the school ecosystem — guaranteeing health, sanitation and a dignified future.
